Toronto Home Inspection: Frequently Asked Questions

Expert answers to the most common questions about GTA real estate inspections, costs, and red flags.

How much does a home inspection cost in Toronto?

The average cost of a home inspection in Toronto typically ranges from $400 to $600. At ASADS, our pricing is competitive and includes a full examination of the structure, HVAC, electrical, and plumbing, plus a bonus Thermal Imaging scan which many cheaper services (like 'cheapest home inspection Toronto' searches) often exclude.

What is the biggest red flag in a home inspection?

The biggest red flags are structural foundation cracks (especially horizontal ones), outdated aluminum wiring, and active mold or water damage. Unlike competitors like Housemaster or Solex, ASADS provides a licensed construction quote alongside our inspection report, so you know exactly how much these "red flags" will cost to fix before you close the deal.

Who pays for a home inspection in Ontario?

In Ontario, the buyer typically pays for the home inspection as part of their due diligence. However, smart sellers are increasingly booking pre-listing inspections to find issues early and avoid "deal-breakers" during negotiations.

How should a seller prepare for an inspection?

Sellers should clean and declutter access points to the furnace, electrical panel, and attic. Ensure all light bulbs are working and the furnace filter is clean. This allows our certified home inspectors to provide an accurate and smooth assessment without delays.

Toronto Real Estate & Home Inspection FAQ

Expert answers on GTA building codes, repair costs, critical red flags, and home inspection best practices for buyers, sellers, and investors.

How much does a home inspection cost in Toronto?

The average cost of a home inspection in Toronto typically ranges from $400 to $600. ASADS provides competitive pricing with a full structural assessment, plus a Thermal Imaging scan to detect hidden moisture and mold.

What is the biggest red flag in a home inspection?

The most serious red flags include horizontal foundation cracks wider than 1/4 inch, outdated aluminum wiring, and active mold or water damage. ASADS provides licensed repair estimates so you know potential costs before purchase.

Who pays for a home inspection in Ontario?

Typically, the buyer pays for the inspection. However, sellers may book a pre-listing inspection to uncover issues early and avoid negotiation delays.

How do I schedule an inspection in Toronto or the GTA?

Call ASADS at 647-801-9311 to book your inspection. We offer urgent same-day bookings and are available Monday through Sunday.

How should a seller prepare for an inspection?

Clean and declutter access points to the furnace, electrical panel, and attic. Ensure light bulbs work and furnace filters are clean to help inspectors deliver an accurate report efficiently.

Does a standard home inspection include mold testing?

Our inspections include Thermal Imaging to detect moisture intrusion and potential mold growth. ASADS identifies the source, like foundation seepage or attic condensation, preventing recurring problems.

Do you inspect for Knob & Tube or Aluminum Wiring?

Yes. These are insurance-critical issues. ASADS identifies them and provides an ESA-compliant repair estimate immediately.

How do I identify Kitec plumbing in a Toronto condo?

Kitec plumbing (blue/orange pipes) was common in GTA condos built 1995-2007. ASADS inspects for these to help with Status Certificate negotiations and future insurance coverage.

When should I book a Tarion Warranty inspection?

Book inspections for new homes in Vaughan, Markham, or Brampton before your 1-Year or 2-Year Tarion deadlines to catch hidden builder defects.

What are the 5 most common home inspection issues in the GTA?

1. Water Damage: Basement seepage, poor grading
2. Roofing: Aging shingles or flashing
3. Electrical: Double-tapped breakers, ungrounded outlets
4. HVAC: End-of-life furnaces, cracked heat exchangers
5. Structural: Sagging joists in older homes

What is the 1% Rule for maintenance?

Set aside 1% of your home's value annually for maintenance. ASADS inspections help identify "Big Ticket" items like roofs and furnaces to budget proactively.

When should I book a post-move-in inspection?

Consider a condition-based check every 3-5 years to catch small issues before they become expensive repairs.
