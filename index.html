Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to what buyers, sellers, and homeowners ask us most about inspection and construction.

How much does a home inspection cost in Ontario, and what is your price?

Home inspection prices vary based on the size, age, and type of property (house, townhouse, vs. condo). Our pricing is competitive and transparent, and we do not charge extra fees for thermal imaging or same-day reports. **For an accurate, no-obligation quote, please call or text us at 647-801-9311 or fill out the contact form below.**

What does an ASADS home inspection include, and is there a checklist?

Our comprehensive visual inspection follows a strict checklist, covering all major systems and components: **Structure, Foundation, Roof, Exterior, Interior, Electrical, Plumbing, HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, Air Conditioning), and Insulation.** We inspect accessible areas and provide a detailed, digital report with photos of any defects found. You are welcome to attend and follow along!

What is the biggest red flag or most common issue that fails a home inspection?

A house cannot technically "fail" an inspection, but the biggest red flags are issues that require immediate, costly repair (and are often things that "fail" a building standard check). These commonly include: **major structural movement/foundation cracks, active roof leaks/failures, faulty or outdated electrical systems (like aluminum or knob and tube wiring), and unaddressed water intrusion in the basement.** We clearly highlight these "deal-breaker" issues in our summary.

Is getting a professional home inspection worth the money?

Absolutely. A professional inspection is a small investment that provides crucial protection. It is worth the money to identify costly repairs before you buy, giving you negotiating power and preparing you for immediate or future maintenance costs. The average cost of a major repair found (e.g., HVAC replacement) is often 5-10 times the cost of the inspection itself. Our service goes further by offering in-house repair solutions to simplify your next steps.

Are your inspectors certified and insured in the Greater Toronto Area?

Yes. All ASADS inspectors are fully certified to provincial standards and carry comprehensive **liability and Errors & Omissions (E&O) insurance**. We adhere to the highest standards of practice to give you peace of mind and protect your investment.

How soon can I book an inspection, and how long does it take?

We often accommodate bookings within **24‚Äì48 hours**, which is essential in a fast-moving GTA market. A standard pre-purchase inspection typically takes **2.5 to 4 hours**, depending on the size and age of the property. Call or text 647-801-9311 for the next available slot.

Should I get a home inspection on a brand-new construction home?

Absolutely. We highly recommend a **New Construction Inspection**. Even brand new homes can have serious defects, missed building code requirements, or incomplete work. An inspection saves you significant money and headaches if defects are documented and addressed **before closing** with the builder under your Tarion warranty.
