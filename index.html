Toronto Home Inspection Frequently Asked Questions

Expert answers to common questions about home inspections in Toronto and the GTA.

How much does a home inspection cost in Toronto and the GTA?

Home inspection costs in Toronto and the GTA typically range from $400 to $700 depending on the size, age, and type of property. Larger homes, older houses, and additional services may increase the cost.

Is a home inspection worth the money?

Yes. A professional home inspection can uncover hidden defects, safety hazards, and costly repairs, helping buyers make informed decisions or renegotiate the purchase price.

Who pays for the home inspection â€” buyer or seller?

In Toronto and across Ontario, the buyer usually pays for the home inspection as part of the due diligence process.

Are home inspections negotiable in price?

Some home inspection pricing may be negotiable depending on property size, location, or bundled services, but quality and experience should be prioritized over price.

What does a home inspection include?

A home inspection includes a visual evaluation of the roof, attic, structure, foundation, electrical, plumbing, HVAC systems, windows, doors, and interior and exterior components.

How long does a home inspection take?

Most home inspections in Toronto take between 2 to 3 hours, depending on the size, age, and condition of the property.

What will fail a home inspection?

Homes do not pass or fail a home inspection. Inspectors report observed defects and safety concerns so buyers can decide how to proceed.

Do home inspectors check electrical, plumbing, and HVAC?

Yes. Home inspectors examine electrical systems, plumbing components, and heating and cooling systems for visible defects and safety issues.

Does a home inspection include the roof and attic?

Yes. Home inspections include a visual inspection of the roof, attic, insulation, and ventilation where safely accessible.

Do inspectors check for mold or asbestos?

Mold and asbestos testing are not included in a standard home inspection but can be added as optional services if concerns are present.

What happens if a home fails inspection?

Homes do not fail inspections. Buyers may choose to renegotiate, request repairs, or withdraw from the purchase if inspection conditions are included.

Can a buyer back out after a home inspection?

Yes. If the offer includes a home inspection condition, buyers in Ontario may withdraw or renegotiate based on inspection findings.

What repairs should a seller make after inspection?

Sellers typically address major safety issues, structural concerns, or code-related defects that may affect the sale.

How serious are home inspection issues?

Issues range from minor maintenance items to major structural or safety concerns. The inspector explains severity so buyers can prioritize repairs.

How do I choose a good home inspector in Toronto?

Choose a certified, insured home inspector with strong reviews, clear reports, and experience inspecting Toronto and GTA properties.

Are home inspectors licensed in Ontario?

Ontario does not require licensing, but professional home inspectors are certified through organizations such as InterNACHI or OAHI.

What qualifications should a home inspector have?

A qualified home inspector should be certified, insured, experienced, and knowledgeable about Ontario building standards.

Should I attend the home inspection?

Yes. Attending allows buyers to ask questions, understand findings, and learn property maintenance tips.

Do condos need a home inspection?

Yes. Condo inspections focus on interior components such as electrical, plumbing, HVAC, appliances, and visible structural elements.

Is a home inspection required for new construction?

Not required, but highly recommended. New homes may still have defects related to workmanship or installation.

Do older homes need inspections more?

Yes. Older homes often have aging systems, outdated wiring, or structural wear that require closer evaluation.

How soon do you get the home inspection report?

Most home inspection reports in Toronto and the GTA are delivered the same day or within 24 hours.

What does a home inspection report look like?

A home inspection report includes photos, descriptions of findings, safety concerns, and recommendations in a clear digital format.

Can a home inspection affect the appraisal?

Indirectly. While appraisals focus on market value, significant inspection findings may influence negotiations.
