What are the most expensive repairs found in Hamilton home inspections?

Answer: Based on our 2024 data, the costliest repairs in Hamilton are: 1) Foundation issues due to escarpment movement ($15,000-$40,000+), 2) Knob-and-tube wiring replacement ($8,000-$15,000), 3) Complete roof replacement ($8,000-$20,000), 4) Sewer line replacement from collapsed clay tiles ($5,000-$15,000), and 5) Full window replacement in heritage homes ($10,000-$25,000). We provide repair cost estimates in our reports for negotiation purposes.

How does Hamilton's steel industry history affect home inspections?

Answer: Hamilton's industrial past creates unique inspection challenges: 1) Accelerated exterior deterioration from historic air pollution affects siding, roofing, and paint on older homes, 2) Galvanized plumbing corrosion is more rapid in industrial-adjacent areas, 3) Soil contamination concerns in certain neighborhoods may require environmental testing, 4) Insurance premiums are higher for homes in former industrial zones, and 5) Heritage designation complications for renovations in historically industrial areas.

Are Hamilton homes more prone to foundation problems than other cities?

Answer: Yes, 3x more likely based on our regional comparison. Hamilton's unique factors include: 1) Escarpment slope movement affecting 40% of hillside properties, 2) Clay soil expansion/contraction causes seasonal foundation movement, 3) High water table in lower city creates hydrostatic pressure, 4) Older stone foundations (pre-1920) lack proper footings, and 5) Poor historical drainage on sloped lots. We use laser levels to detect movement as small as 1/4 inch.

What insurance companies require after a Hamilton home inspection?

Answer: For 2024, Hamilton insurers typically require: 1) Knob-and-tube wiring removal (all major companies), 2) Electrical panel replacement if Federal Pacific or Zinsco, 3) Backwater valve installation in lower city flood zones, 4) Oil tank removal certification, 5) Roof certification if over 15 years old, 6) Wood stove WETT inspection, and 7) Updated plumbing if galvanized. We provide insurance-specific reports that satisfy these requirements.

Do you inspect illegal basement apartments in Hamilton?

Many Hamilton homes have these units - we help buyers understand risks and costs.
