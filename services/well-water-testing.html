Common Well Water Contaminants in Ontario

Health risks associated with untreated well water contamination

High Risk

E.coli & Coliform Bacteria

Indicates fecal contamination. Causes gastrointestinal illness, diarrhea, vomiting. Especially dangerous for children.

High Risk

Nitrates (NO3)

From agricultural runoff. Causes methemoglobinemia (blue baby syndrome) in infants under 6 months.

Moderate Risk

Arsenic

Naturally occurring in bedrock. Long-term exposure causes skin lesions, cancer, cardiovascular disease.

Moderate Risk

Lead

From old plumbing. Causes neurological damage, developmental delays in children, hypertension in adults.

Low Risk

Iron & Manganese

Causes staining, metallic taste. Not health hazardous but affects water quality and plumbing systems.

Low Risk

Sodium & Chloride

From road salt runoff. Concerns for sodium-restricted diets. Can corrode plumbing and appliances.

Ontario Well Water Testing Regulations

Legal requirements and recommended testing frequencies

Mandatory

Real Estate Transactions

Mortgage lenders require bacterial water test (E.coli/coliform) for private well properties. Must be conducted by accredited laboratory within 30 days of closing.

Every 6 Months

Bacteria Testing

Public Health Ontario recommends testing for E.coli and total coliform bacteria every 6 months, especially after heavy rainfall or flooding.

Every 2 Years

Chemical Analysis

Comprehensive chemical testing for nitrates, arsenic, lead, uranium, and other parameters every 2 years or if water quality changes.

As Needed

Special Circumstances

Test after well repair, new well construction, nearby septic system failure, or if family members experience gastrointestinal illness.

Well Water Testing FAQ

Common questions about well water testing, Ontario regulations, and health risks

How often should I test my well water in Ontario?
Public Health Ontario recommends testing for bacteria every 6 months (spring and fall). Chemical testing should be conducted every 2 years. Test immediately if water changes color, taste, or odor; after well repair; after flooding; or if family members experience gastrointestinal illness. Real estate transactions require testing within 30 days of closing.
What does a positive E.coli test mean?
A positive E.coli test indicates fecal contamination in your well water. Immediate action is required: boil water for at least 1 minute before drinking, cooking, or brushing teeth. The well must be shocked with chlorine, retested after 48 hours, and the source of contamination identified and corrected. Continuous E.coli presence may require well replacement or permanent treatment system installation.
Is well water testing mandatory for real estate in Ontario?
While not legally mandated by the province, most mortgage lenders require a satisfactory bacterial water test (E.coli and coliform) for properties with private wells. The test must be conducted by an Ontario Ministry of Health accredited laboratory within 30 days of closing. Without this, financing may be denied. Additional chemical testing is increasingly required, especially in agricultural areas.
