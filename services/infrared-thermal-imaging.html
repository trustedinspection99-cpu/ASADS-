What is Thermal Imaging in Home Inspection?

Infrared Thermography (IRT) is the science of capturing and analyzing heat signatures emitted by objects. In a home inspection context, our **FLIRÂ® high-resolution cameras** allow us to visualize temperature variations across surfaces like drywall, ceilings, and floors. Because moisture, air, and electricity all change the temperature of the materials they interact with, we can "see" through the surface layer to diagnose deep-seated problems.

At ASADS, we integrate thermal imaging into every comprehensive inspection to ensure no defect is left hidden. This is the difference between a "visual" check and a professional technical audit.

1. Moisture Intrusion & Hidden Leak Detection

Water has a high "thermal mass," meaning it retains heat differently than wood or drywall. Wet spots typically appear as cooler (darker) anomalies on our screens due to **evaporative cooling**. We use this to detect:

  • Active Roof Leaks: Tracking water travel from shingles through attic insulation.
  • Plumbing Failures: Pinpointing slow drips behind showers, toilets, and kitchen cabinetry.
  • Foundation Seepage: Identifying water ingress behind finished basement walls and under flooring.

2. Electrical Hotspot & Fire Risk Mitigation

Electrical resistance creates heat. An overloaded circuit, a loose wire nut, or a failing breaker will "glow" on our thermal scans before it ever trips a fuse or causes a fire. This is vital for homes with **Aluminum wiring** or old **Federal Pacific/Zinsco panels**, where connections frequently loosen over time.

3. Building Envelope & Energy Efficiency Audits

In the harsh Ontario winters, heat loss is the primary driver of high utility bills. We perform a full scan of your building envelope to identify:

  • Thermal Bridging: Where structural studs transfer cold air directly into your home.
  • Insulation Voids: Missing or settled insulation in wall cavities and attic corners.
  • Air Leakage: Drafts around window sashes, door frames, and electrical outlets.

Why Professional Interpretation Matters

A thermal camera is a powerful tool, but it requires a **Certified Thermographer** to interpret the data. Not every cold spot is a leak; sometimes it's simply a shadow or a different material density.
