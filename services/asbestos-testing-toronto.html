Where Asbestos is Found in Toronto Homes

If your home was built before 1990, these materials likely contain asbestos

High Risk

Vermiculite Insulation

Common in attics of homes built 1919-1990. Zonolite brand contains tremolite asbestos fibers.

High Risk

Popcorn Ceilings

Acoustic ceilings (1970s-1980s) contain up to 10% asbestos. Crumbling creates airborne fibers.

Moderate Risk

Electrical Wiring

Old wiring insulation contains asbestos. Disturbing during renovations releases fibers.

Moderate Risk

Pipe Insulation

White corrugated paper wrap on basement pipes often contains chrysotile asbestos.

Low Risk

Vinyl Floor Tiles

9"x9" tiles (1950s-1980s) contain asbestos. Risk increases when sanding or removing.

Low Risk

Furnace Duct Tape

Old cloth duct tape contains asbestos fibers that become airborne when deteriorating.

Ontario Asbestos Regulations & Bans

Understanding the legal requirements for asbestos testing and removal

2018

Ontario's Asbestos Ban (Bill 182)

Complete ban on asbestos use in new construction and renovations. Mandatory asbestos testing required before demolition or renovation of pre-1990 buildings.

2015

Enhanced OHSA Regulations

Workplace Safety & Insurance Board (WSIB) requires asbestos awareness training for all construction workers. Strict exposure limits established.

1990

Asbestos Products Ban

Canada bans most asbestos-containing products. Homes built after 1990 are generally asbestos-free.

Asbestos Testing FAQ

Common questions about asbestos, mesothelioma prevention, and Ontario regulations

When is asbestos testing required in Ontario?
Under Ontario Regulation 278/05, asbestos testing is MANDATORY before any renovation or demolition work on buildings constructed before 1990. This includes home renovations, demolition of any pre-1990 structure, commercial building renovations, and school or hospital renovations.
How much does asbestos testing cost in Toronto?
Professional asbestos testing in Toronto ranges from $299-$599+HST depending on the scope. Basic single-material testing starts at $299, while comprehensive whole-home inspection is $599. Asbestos removal ranges from $1,500-$15,000 depending on material amount and complexity.
What happens if asbestos is found in my home?
If asbestos is found, you have three options: 1) Leave it undisturbed if in good condition and not being renovated, 2) Encapsulate it with sealants (for minor amounts), or 3) Complete removal by WSIB-certified professionals (required for renovations).
