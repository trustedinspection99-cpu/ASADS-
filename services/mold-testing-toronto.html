Mold Testing FAQ

Common questions about mold testing, health concerns, and laboratory analysis

How much does mold testing cost in Toronto?
Mold testing in Toronto ranges from $349-$999 depending on the scope. Basic air sampling starts at $349+HST, while comprehensive PCR analysis with multiple samples averages $699+HST. Commercial and multi-unit testing starts at $999+HST. This investment can identify health risks and prevent costly remediation issues.
How long does mold testing take?
On-site sampling takes 1-2 hours. Laboratory analysis typically takes 3-5 business days for standard analysis. We offer 48-hour rush service for urgent health concerns at an additional fee. Same-day preliminary results are available for immediate health risk assessment.
What's the difference between air and surface sampling?
Air sampling measures airborne spore counts to assess what you're breathing, while surface sampling identifies mold growing on specific materials. Air sampling detects hidden mold in walls or HVAC systems, while surface sampling confirms visible growth. We recommend both methods for comprehensive assessment.
Do you provide remediation recommendations?
Yes, our reports include specific remediation protocols based on mold type and contamination level. We provide detailed removal procedures, containment requirements, and post-remediation verification testing. We can also recommend certified mold remediation contractors and oversee the remediation process.
Is black mold testing different from regular mold testing?
Yes, black mold (Stachybotrys) requires specific PCR testing for accurate identification. Standard spore count analysis may miss or misidentify black mold. Our PCR testing specifically identifies Stachybotrys and 35+ other mold species, providing accurate results for toxic mold concerns.
Are your lab results accepted by insurance companies?
Yes, our AIHA-accredited laboratory results are accepted by all major insurance companies for mold damage claims. We provide court-admissible documentation that can be used for insurance disputes, legal proceedings, and real estate transactions.
