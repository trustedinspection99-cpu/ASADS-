Expert Suites Inspections: Beyond the Surface

Many buyers assume that because a condo is newer or "managed," an inspection is unnecessary. However, **condo-specific defects** like faulty Fan Coil Units (HVAC), aging kitec plumbing, or window seal failures can cost thousands in unexpected "Special Assessments" or repair bills.

What We Inspect in Your Suite:

  • HVAC / Fan Coil Units: Testing for proper heating/cooling, filter health, and condensate drain leaks.
  • Thermal Imaging: Scanning walls for hidden moisture from neighboring units and checking window energy efficiency.
  • Plumbing: Inspecting all shut-off valves, laundry connections, and checking for "Kitec" or recalled piping.
  • Electrical: Testing outlets, GFCI protection in kitchens/baths, and the main suite panel.
  • Appliances: Full functional check of the stove, dishwasher, fridge, washer, and dryer.
  • Balcony & Windows: Inspecting railings, glass seals, and door hardware for safety and drafts.

Answering Real Condo Buyer Questions

Does the inspector check the building's roof?

For standard suite inspections, we focus on the unit. However, we review the Status Certificate (if provided) for mentions of major building issues like roof or parking garage leaks.

What is the most common issue found in Toronto condos?

Slow leaks behind laundry machines and faulty HVAC actuators (which control your heat/AC) are the top defects we uncover in GTA high-rises.

Can you detect leaks from the unit above me?

Yes. Using Infrared Thermal Imaging, we scan your ceilings for "cold spots" that indicate active water leaks from neighboring units.
