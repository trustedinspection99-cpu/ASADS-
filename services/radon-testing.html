Radon Testing FAQ

Common questions about radon gas, testing, and lung cancer prevention

What is radon gas and why is it dangerous?
Radon is a radioactive gas that comes from the natural breakdown of uranium in soil, rock, and water. It's odorless, colorless, and tasteless. When inhaled, radon particles decay in your lungs, releasing radiation that damages lung tissue and can cause lung cancer. According to Health Canada, radon is the #1 cause of lung cancer in non-smokers, responsible for over 3,200 Canadian deaths annually. Prolonged exposure to elevated radon levels significantly increases lung cancer risk.
How much does radon testing cost in Toronto?
Professional radon testing in Toronto ranges from $199-$299+HST depending on the testing method. Short-term screening tests start at $199, while comprehensive long-term testing with digital monitoring is $299. Radon mitigation system installation ranges from $1,500-$3,500 depending on home size and foundation type. Health Canada recommends professional testing over DIY kits for accurate results that meet national guidelines.
What is the Health Canada radon guideline?
Health Canada's radon guideline is 200 becquerels per cubic meter (Bq/m¬≥). This is the maximum acceptable annual average radon concentration in residential dwellings. If your home tests above 200 Bq/m¬≥, Health Canada recommends taking action to reduce radon levels. Between 200-600 Bq/m¬≥, remediation should be done within two years. Above 600 Bq/m¬≥, remediation should be done within one year due to significantly higher lung cancer risk.
How long does radon testing take?
Health Canada recommends long-term testing for a minimum of 91 days (3 months) to get an accurate annual average radon level, as radon concentrations fluctuate daily and seasonally. We also offer short-term tests (2-7 days) for preliminary screening and real estate transactions. For the most accurate assessment of your lung cancer risk, long-term testing is essential. We provide same-day preliminary results with our continuous monitoring equipment.
Can radon be reduced if my home has high levels?
Yes, radon can be effectively reduced by 90%+ with professional mitigation systems. The most common and effective method is Active Soil Depressurization (ASD), which involves installing PVC piping through your foundation with an inline fan to vent radon gas safely outside before it enters your home. Professional mitigation typically costs $1,500-$3,500 and includes post-mitigation testing to verify radon reduction below Health Canada guidelines.
Do I need radon testing for a real estate transaction?
While not legally required in Ontario, radon testing is increasingly becoming a standard part of home inspections, especially for basement suites and newer energy-efficient homes. Many buyers now request radon testing as a condition of sale. We provide fast 2-7 day tests for real estate transactions with detailed reports that can be used for negotiation or remediation requests. Post-mitigation testing is also available to verify system effectiveness.
