Where Lead Paint is Found in Toronto Homes

Homes built before 1990 contain lead paint in these high-risk areas

High Risk

Windows & Doors

Friction surfaces create lead dust through opening/closing. Highest concentration of lead paint found on windowsills.

High Risk

Baseboards & Trim

Original woodwork contains lead paint. Sanding or scraping during renovations creates airborne lead dust.

Moderate Risk

Exterior Siding

Weathering exterior lead paint contaminates soil around home foundation. Children ingest through hand-to-mouth activity.

Moderate Risk

Radiators & Pipes

Metal surfaces painted with lead-based paints. Heat accelerates deterioration and dust creation.

Low Risk

Wall Surfaces

Lead paint under layers of newer paint. Risk occurs during demolition, sanding, or wall repair activities.

Low Risk

Cabinets & Built-ins

Original kitchen/bathroom cabinets often contain lead paint. Risk during renovation or refinishing projects.

Ontario Lead Paint Regulations & Bans

Legal requirements for lead paint testing and safe removal in Ontario

2011

EPA RRP Rule Adoption

Ontario adopts US EPA Renovation, Repair and Painting Rule. Certification required for work in pre-1978 homes. Lead-safe work practices mandatory.

2005

Consumer Product Safety Act

Canada bans lead in consumer paints exceeding 90 ppm. Existing lead paint in older homes remains legal but hazardous during disturbance.

1990

Lead Paint Phase-Out

Voluntary industry phase-out of lead-based residential paints. Homes built after 1990 generally lead-paint free.

1976

Lead Content Reduction

Maximum lead content reduced to 0.5% by weight. Pre-1976 paint contains highest lead concentrations (up to 50%).

Lead Paint Testing FAQ

Common questions about lead paint, child safety, and Ontario regulations

How do I know if my home has lead paint?
Homes built before 1990 have a high probability of containing lead paint. Pre-1978 homes almost certainly contain lead paint. The only reliable method is professional XRF testing or laboratory paint chip analysis. DIY test kits produce false negatives and are not reliable. Visual inspection cannot detect lead paint - it looks identical to non-lead paint.
Is lead paint testing required for renovations in Ontario?
Ontario Regulation 278/05 requires lead paint testing before renovation, repair, or painting in pre-1978 homes. Contractors must be EPA RRP certified and follow lead-safe work practices. Testing is mandatory for: window replacement, exterior painting, demolition of painted surfaces, and any work that disturbs more than 1 square foot of painted surface per room.
What are the health effects of lead paint exposure?
Children under 6 are most vulnerable: permanent neurological damage, reduced IQ (4-7 points per 10 Œºg/dL), learning disabilities, behavioral problems, hearing loss, and slowed growth. Pregnant women: miscarriage, premature birth, fetal brain damage. Adults: hypertension, kidney damage, reproductive problems, memory/concentration issues. No safe blood lead level exists.
