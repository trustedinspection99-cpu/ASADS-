Sewer Camera Inspection FAQ

Common questions about sewer scope inspections and pre-purchase protection

What is a sewer camera inspection and why do I need one?
A sewer camera inspection uses a waterproof CCTV camera to visually inspect the interior of your sewer line from the house to the municipal connection. It's essential for home buyers because standard home inspections don't check sewer lines. Hidden defects like root intrusion, bellied pipes, or cracks can cost $5,000-$25,000+ to repair after purchase. Our inspection provides video proof of the sewer line condition for negotiation or repair requests.
How much does a sewer camera inspection cost in Toronto?
Sewer camera inspections in Toronto range from $299-$499 depending on property size and location. Our standard residential inspection is $299+HST, while our premium HD inspection with GPS mapping is $399+HST. Commercial and emergency inspections start at $499+HST. This small investment can save you from $5,000-$25,000+ in unexpected sewer repairs after purchase.
How long does a sewer inspection take?
A typical sewer camera inspection takes 1-2 hours. We provide same-day digital reports with HD video footage of the entire inspection. The camera travels up to 200 feet to reach the municipal connection point. We inspect the entire length from the house cleanout to the city main, identifying any defects along the way.
Can you inspect during winter or bad weather?
Yes, we perform sewer camera inspections year-round including winter months. Our equipment is designed for all weather conditions, and the camera operates in water, so rain or snow doesn't affect the inspection quality. We can inspect in temperatures as low as -20¬∞C, making us one of the few companies offering reliable winter sewer inspections in Toronto.
What if you find problems with the sewer line?
If we find defects, we provide detailed repair recommendations and cost estimates. We can connect you with certified sewer repair contractors and provide the video evidence needed for negotiations with sellers. Many clients use our inspection reports to request repairs or price reductions before closing. We also offer re-inspection services after repairs are completed.
Do you provide the video footage?
Yes, we provide full HD video recordings of the entire sewer inspection. The video is delivered digitally the same day and can be shared with real estate agents, lawyers, or contractors. The footage shows real-time inspection with audio commentary from our certified inspector, clearly identifying any defects and their locations.
