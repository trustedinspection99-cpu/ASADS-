Markham Home Inspection FAQ ‚Äì Current 2024 Questions

What are common issues in Markham's luxury estate homes ($2M+)?

Answer: Markham luxury homes often have: 1) Complex roof systems with multiple valleys and flashing points requiring specialized inspection, 2) High-end appliance integration issues (professional ranges, built-in refrigerators), 3) Advanced HVAC zoning system malfunctions in multi-level homes, 4) In-ground sprinkler and irrigation system leaks and controller issues, 5) Smart home system integration problems between different manufacturers, and 6) Poor drainage on large lots with complex grading. We specialize in these premium property inspections with appropriate testing equipment.

How do Markham's strict building codes affect inspections?

Answer: Markham has some of Ontario's strictest codes requiring specific attention to: 1) Backwater valve requirements in all new builds (post-2010), 2) Higher insulation standards (R50+ attics vs provincial R40), 3) Specific foundation waterproofing requirements for basement living spaces, 4) Advanced fire separation in townhomes and multi-unit dwellings, and 5) Energy efficiency standards exceeding provincial requirements. We verify compliance with all Markham-specific bylaws and provide correction recommendations.

Are there special considerations for Markham's infill properties?

Answer: Markham infill properties (tear-down rebuilds in established areas) require attention to: 1) Sewer line connections to older municipal systems that may need upgrading, 2) Proper lot grading considering neighboring older homes with settled foundations, 3) Foundation depth matching surrounding properties to prevent water issues, 4) Heritage district restrictions in Unionville/Markham Village affecting exterior materials, and 5) Setback compliance on narrower lots in established neighborhoods. We have specific checklists for infill inspections.

What should I know about buying investment properties in Markham?

Answer: Markham investment properties require specialized inspection for: 1) Legal basement apartment verification (City of Markham permits and compliance), 2) Rental property maintenance condition assessment for tenant turnover planning, 3) Utility separation for multiple units (hydro, water meters), 4) Fire code compliance for multi-family dwellings (egress, smoke alarms, fire separation), and 5) Parking and access requirements for multiple tenants. We provide investment-specific reports with ROI analysis for landlords.

How do you inspect homes with complex additions in established neighborhoods?

Answer: Markham's established neighborhoods often have complex additions. We systematically check: 1) Proper permitting and engineering for structural modifications (foundation extensions, roof alterations), 2) Integration of new and old HVAC systems and potential capacity issues, 3) Roof connection flashing and waterproofing at addition junctions, 4) Foundation settlement differences between old and new sections, and 5) Electrical panel capacity for added square footage and circuits. We trace addition history through municipal records when available.
