Tarion Warranty Timeline & Requirements

Understanding Ontario's new home warranty program deadlines and coverage periods

Pre-Closing

Pre-Delivery Inspection (PDI)

Mandatory inspection before closing. Builder must document all deficiencies. You have 7 days after PDI to identify additional items. Missing this inspection can void warranty claims.

30 Days

30-Day Form Submission

Critical deadline: 30 days after closing. All construction deficiencies must be documented and submitted to Tarion. After 30 days, most items are no longer covered under warranty.

1 Year

1-Year Form Submission

Submit 1-year form 30 days before 1st anniversary. Covers workmanship and materials defects. Builder has 120 days to repair items. After 1 year, only major structural defects covered.

2 Years

2-Year Warranty Expiry

Major structural defects coverage ends at 2 years. After this point, homeowners bear full cost of repairs. Proper documentation during first two years is essential for claims.

Tarion Warranty Inspection FAQ

Common questions about Tarion inspections, deadlines, and Ontario new home warranties

What is the difference between PDI and 30-Day Form inspection?
The Pre-Delivery Inspection (PDI) occurs before closing and documents any deficiencies present at that time. The 30-Day Form inspection happens after you move in (within 30 days of closing) and identifies issues that become apparent with daily use. Both are critical - the PDI sets baseline conditions, while the 30-Day Form captures living-in deficiencies. Missing either can void warranty coverage.
What happens if I miss the 30-day Tarion deadline?
If you miss the 30-day deadline for submitting your Tarion 30-Day Form, you lose warranty coverage for most construction deficiencies. Only "material" defects (significant structural issues) may still be covered, but even these claims become more difficult. Tarion strictly enforces deadlines - extensions are rarely granted. Professional inspection ensures timely, complete submission.
How many deficiencies do new homes typically have?
Industry data shows 87% of new homes have 15+ construction deficiencies. Common issues include drywall imperfections, paint defects, improper caulking, electrical code violations, HVAC installation errors, and grading/drainage problems. Professional inspection identifies all deficiencies for proper warranty claims, preventing costly post-warranty repairs.
