The ASADS Master Checklist: What We Inspect

Roofing & Attic Systems

We evaluate shingles, flashings, chimneys, and skylights. Inside the attic, we check for proper ventilation, "black mold" signs, and R-value insulation levels to ensure energy efficiency.

Foundation & Structural Integrity

We scan for horizontal and vertical cracks, foundation settlement, sagging floor joists, and moisture penetration in basements or crawlspaces.

Electrical Review

From the main service mast to the breaker panel. We identify dangerous **Knob-and-Tube wiring**, **Aluminum wiring**, ungrounded outlets, and double-tapped breakers.

Plumbing & Drainage

We check for old galvanized pipes, recalled Kitec plumbing, sewage back-up risks, and verify water pressure and hot water tank safety.

HVAC Performance

Functional testing of gas furnaces, air conditioners, and heat pumps. We provide an estimated "remaining life" for these expensive mechanical units.

Building Envelope

Inspection of windows, siding, soffits, and brickwork. We check for proper grading to ensure water flows away from your foundation.

Advanced Infrared Thermal Imaging Included

Standard visual inspections often miss what's happening behind the drywall. That is why **every pre-purchase inspection includes Thermal Imaging**. Our infrared cameras detect:

  • Hidden roof and plumbing leaks before they cause visible water stains.
  • Cold spots that indicate missing or shifted wall insulation.
  • Overheating electrical circuits that pose a fire risk.
  • Moisture intrusion in basement walls that leads to mold growth.

Real-World FAQs for Toronto Home Buyers

Is a home inspection worth it for a "Newer" home?
Absolutely. We frequently find major venting issues, electrical hazards, and improper grading even in homes less than 5 years old in areas like Brampton and Vaughan.

How long does the inspection take?
A typical detached home takes 2.5 to 3.5 hours. We encourage you to attend so we can walk you through the maintenance requirements of your new home.

What if the home has a finished basement?
Finished basements often hide foundation cracks. We use moisture meters and thermal cameras to "look through" the finishes and ensure you aren't buying a flooded basement waiting to happen.
